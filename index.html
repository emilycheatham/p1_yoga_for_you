Why Practice Yoga?

Mental Benefits

Yoga focuses on breathing and focusing on the present, this helps with managing stress, reducing symptoms of anxiety and stress, and can improve sleep.

Physical Benefits

Through stretching and flexing different muscles groups, yoga builds whole body strength. This not only improves the practitioners flexibility but can also improve muscle strength, posture and balance. It also helps to prevent injuries such as cartilage and joint breakdown.

Health Benefits

Along with cardio and circulatory health, which aids our heart and breathing, regularly practicing yoga helps to balance the nervous system, which helps to energise and heal the body. This help prevent IBS and digestive problems.

Ability

Yoga is fully inclusive. I can be made suitable to all abilities and adaptive to all needs. Yoga can be practiced individually or as part of a group, in any location, and at any time. No resources are required, they are only used to enhance practice.

What Types of Yoga Do We Offer?

Align

Focusing on the correct alignment of the body, this style involves holding postures for several breaths. A great class for building strength and balance.

Hatha

Slower than other yoga styles, focusing on breathing and holding pose for longer. Ideal for all levels, but a great place for start for beginners

Vinyasa

Focusing on coordinating breath to flow from one pose to the other. It is similar in style to Hatha but at a quicker pace

Yin

A slow-paced style of yoga with seated positions which are held for long periods of time. A great glass to help you find inner peace.

Flow

Dynamic fast paced posed, linked together with breath. No set sequences of poses, sequences are down to the teacher's creativity. A great class for lifting heart rates and spirits.

Prenatal classes

Tailored to pregnant people in all trimesters. Carefully adapted excerises to ensure safety for all those involved. Gentle exercised aimed at strengthening muscles and developing breathing techniques.

Our Event Classes

Hot yoga

Making the room hot and humid. Hot yoga allows for your muscles to be warmer, allowing for more flexibility and high calory burn. Make sure you bring a towel and lots of water for this.

Pet yoga

Pupyy Yoga, Kitten Yoga and Bunny Yoga Working with ethical breeders, pet yoga combining yoga and pets in a stress-free environment. It allows for the animals to socialise before entering the outside world.

Aerial yoga

Experience weightlessness! A hybrid yoga, combining traditional yoga poses with the use of an aerial hammock. Allows access to a wider range of movements that would otherwise be restricted. Ideal for a cardio workout.
